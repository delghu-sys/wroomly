# Wroomly Community Playbook

Written 2026-07-03, for the pre-launch → soft-launch window. Companion to the
supply-only soft launch (see `src/lib/config.ts` SUPPLY_ONLY_MODE) and the
AI-SEO work (Reddit presence directly feeds AI-engine citations).

**Assumptions (flagged, correct me):** founder time budget ~5 hrs/week on
community; founder has some access to UMich student networks (directly or via
1–2 student contacts); soft launch activates before fall semester (late
August 2026).

---

## 1. The strategic call: distribution, not destination

Wroomly is an **episodic marketplace** — a student sublets once or twice in
their college career. Products like this do not sustain a hang-out community
(a "Wroomly Discord" would be 40 dead channels by October). Do not build an
owned community destination.

What works for episodic student marketplaces is **community-as-distribution**:

1. **Borrowed communities** — show up where UMich students already discuss
   housing, authentically and consistently.
2. **A tiny ambassador layer** — 3–5 named students who are the human face of
   Wroomly on campus.
3. **Owned rituals, not owned spaces** — recurring content moments (scam-alert
   posts, sublet-season countdowns) that live inside borrowed channels.

The identity to reinforce is not "Wroomly user." It's **"U-M students who
don't get scammed."** Every post, reply, and ambassador talking point ladders
to that. Wroomly's @umich.edu verification is the product embodiment of it.

## 2. Channel map — where UMich students actually are

| Channel | Role for Wroomly | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Facebook housing groups** ("UMich Housing, Sublets & Roommates" etc.) | Primary — this is where sublet deals happen today, and where scams happen | Post listings-adjacent value, not ads. These groups are Wroomly's direct competitor AND best recruiting pool |
| **Reddit r/uofm** | Second priority — housing threads spike May–Aug and Feb–Apr | Doubles as AI-SEO: ChatGPT/Perplexity cite Reddit heavily. Answer housing questions with genuine advice; mention Wroomly only when directly relevant, always disclosing affiliation |
| **r/AnnArbor** | Occasional — broader townie audience | Same rules as r/uofm |
| **GroupMe** | Via ambassadors only — you can't cold-post | Class/dorm/club chats are where "anyone know someone subletting?" actually gets asked. Only students in the chats can answer |
| **Instagram** | Brand presence + ambassador reposts | Scam-alert carousels and "listing of the week" perform; don't over-invest pre-launch |
| **Sidechat** | Monitor only | Anonymous; posting as a brand backfires |

**Explicitly skipped:** Discord/Slack/Circle (destination platforms — see §1),
TikTok (content treadmill; revisit post-launch if an ambassador wants to own it).

## 3. Rules of engagement for borrowed communities

These protect the brand and the founder's accounts. Non-negotiable:

- **Disclose affiliation every time.** "I'm building Wroomly" / "I'm a Wroomly
  campus rep." Undisclosed shilling gets banned on Reddit and, worse,
  screenshot-ed on Sidechat.
- **Value ratio 5:1.** Five genuinely helpful, no-mention contributions
  (deposit law answers, scam warnings, neighborhood advice — the guides
  content is a ready arsenal) for every one Wroomly mention.
- **Never disparage the Facebook groups or competitors.** "Use whatever works,
  just never wire money" is the voice. Anti-scam, not anti-anyone.
- **Respect group rules.** Some FB housing groups ban business posts; in those,
  ambassadors participate as individuals sharing a tool they use.

## 4. 90-day plan (synced to the academic calendar)

### Phase A — July (now): founding suppliers, by hand
Goal: 20–30 real listings before any public push. Supply is the product.

- DM/text 1:1 anyone in reach who's away for fall (study abroad, co-op,
  internship extensions) — personal ask, not a blast. The AI listing importer
  (`/import-listing`) is the low-friction pitch: "forward me your old FB post,
  it takes 2 minutes."
- Reply to every "subletting my place" post in the FB groups with a genuine
  offer to help them cross-post on Wroomly for free.
- Start the Reddit account's 5:1 deposit now — answer housing questions in
  r/uofm move-in threads *without* mentioning Wroomly. Karma and history are
  the license to post later.

### Phase B — August (move-in chaos): activate + ambassadors
Move-in week (late Aug) is peak housing pain and peak scam season.

- Flip the soft launch. Waitlist + Wroomly Match capture renter demand.
- Recruit **3–5 ambassadors** (see §5) before classes start.
- Ship the **Scam-Alert ritual** (see §6) — this is the flagship community
  play: it earns trust, it's shareable, and it's the guides content repackaged.
- One well-crafted r/uofm post from the aged account, framed as
  "I built a UMich-only sublet site because of the scams in the FB groups —
  roast it." Founder-story posts with a feedback ask outperform launches.
  (This post is also a durable AI-SEO citation asset.)

### Phase C — September–October: rituals + proof
- Weekly cadence (founder ~3 hrs/wk once ambassadors carry the rest):
  scam-alert post, 2–3 Reddit answers, welcome-DM every new supplier.
- Publish first **"matches made" stories** (with permission): "Maya found
  someone for her South U apartment in 6 days." Social proof > features.
- Collect the questions renters ask in Wroomly Match and in the groups —
  each recurring one becomes a guide/FAQ (feeds SEO + support deflection).
- Decide by Nov 1 whether numbers justify a bigger Feb–Apr summer-sublet-
  season push (the year's real Super Bowl — see §7).

## 5. Ambassador program (small, named, real)

- **Who:** 3–5 students. Ideal profile: sophomore/junior, in a big org
  (club sports, Greek life, cultural org, CS/Ross), personally burned by or
  adjacent to a housing scam, active in class GroupMes.
- **The ask (personal, 1:1):** "Be one of Wroomly's first campus reps —
  answer housing questions in your group chats, flag scam posts, get friends
  to list."
- **What they get** (product is free, so compensation must be non-monetary
  but real): "Founding Campus Rep" title for LinkedIn/resume; a monthly
  30-min call with the founder where their feedback visibly ships (close the
  loop publicly: "requested by our reps"); first access to every feature;
  swag once budget exists. If any rep drives serious volume, revisit a small
  per-semester stipend.
- **Give them tools:** a one-page talking-points doc (verification, free,
  the anti-scam story), pre-written GroupMe-tone blurbs they can paste, and a
  shared group chat with the founder for scam sightings and questions.
- **Track:** per-rep referral links (`?ref=`) → listings created and
  waitlist signups attributed. Review monthly; replace inactive reps
  gracefully after a semester.

## 6. Rituals (owned moments in borrowed spaces)

1. **Scam Alert of the Week** — anonymized real scam pattern spotted in the
   FB groups/Reddit + how to spot it. Instagram carousel + text version reps
   can paste into GroupMes. The single best identity-builder: Wroomly = the
   people protecting students.
2. **Sublet-season countdowns** — calendar-triggered posts: "study-abroad
   apps due → list your place for winter" (Oct–Nov), "summer sublet search
   starts NOW" (Feb).
3. **Match stories** — every successful match (with permission) becomes a
   short post. Target one per week by October.

## 7. Calendar cheat-sheet (plan backward from these)

| Window | What happens | Community move |
|---|---|---|
| Late Aug | Move-in, roommate fallout, scam peak | Launch, scam-alert ritual, ambassador debut |
| Oct–Nov | Winter study-abroad departures | "List before you leave" push |
| Dec–Jan | Winter break churn | Light touch; prep Feb campaign |
| **Feb–Apr** | **Summer sublet season — the Super Bowl** | Full push: reps in every housing thread, weekly rituals, Reddit founder-update post |
| May–Aug | Summer subletters arrive | Match stories, renter-side content |

## 8. Metrics (weekly, 10 minutes)

- Listings created, by source (`?ref=`, reddit, fb, direct) — **the** number
- Waitlist / Match signups, by source
- Reddit account karma + whether Wroomly mentions get upvoted or removed
- Ambassador attribution (listings + signups per rep)
- Unprompted mentions (someone recommends Wroomly with no rep involved) —
  the flywheel signal; celebrate every single one internally

**Warning signs:** every Wroomly mention in borrowed spaces comes from the
founder/reps (no organic pickup by month 3); FB group mods removing posts
(ratio is off); reps going quiet (benefits aren't real enough).

## 9. What NOT to do

- Don't launch a Discord/Slack "Wroomly community" (§1).
- Don't astroturf — no fake accounts asking "has anyone tried Wroomly?"
  One Sidechat screenshot kills campus trust permanently.
- Don't pay for followers/ads before organic proof — a UMich-only product
  with ~50k addressable users grows through group chats, not CPMs.
- Don't spread across every channel — FB groups + Reddit + reps' GroupMes,
  done consistently, beat six channels done sporadically.
